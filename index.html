Paris Nanterre University

Biography

I am an Associate Professor of Computer Science since September 2022 at Paris Nanterre University where I teach all about databases at the Economics, Management, Mathematics and Computer Science department (SEGMI).

From 2017 to 2022, I was at Isep where I was the head of the Information Systems track and program director of the Data Protection Officer Master and and Scientific Collaborator at Inria, MiMove team. Before joining Isep, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Inria, MiMove team.

I obtained a Ph.D. in Computer Science from the University Paris Dauphine - PSL Research University in December 2015 and a Master’s Degree in Computer Science from the Simon Bolivar University. I have participated in the following projects: EIT Digital Civic Tech and Participatory Democracy, Inria Technological development assisting research for SocialBus, IPL Inria@SilliconValley, and EIT Digital CivicBudget and head of the chair between United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP) and Isep. I have also been a visiting scholar at the University of Leicester (2014) and UC Berkeley (2016).

The general goal of my research is to improve the way we deal with data in systems that are heterogeneous, distributed and dynamic; for example, dealing with distributed transactions, interoperability and data integration.

You can send me a message if you want to work or do a Ph.D. with me.
